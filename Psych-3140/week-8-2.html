meta-cognitive processes in using memory

Meta-cognition is a collective name for phenomena that have to do with the mind's perception of its own internal states. Some examples:
  • tip of the tongue (TOT) — almost having the answer to a question;
  • feeling of knowing (FOK) — having high confidence in knowing or not knowing the answer, even when the explicit answer itself is unavailable or not required by the task;
  • ease of learning (EOL) — the anticipation of the ability to learn a task;
  • the Dunning-Kruger effect — [an example from a 1999 study] "participants scoring in the bottom quartile on tests of humor, grammar, and logic grossly overestimated their test performance and ability. Although test scores put them in the 12th percentile, they estimated themselves to be in the 62nd."

This indicates that the monitoring process has no privileged source of data ("folder" listing) to go by. Rather, both retrieval and monitoring are fed by the same pool of information (LTM, or long-term memory).
